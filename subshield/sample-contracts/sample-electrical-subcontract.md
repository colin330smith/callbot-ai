## ARTICLE 1: SCOPE OF WORK

Subcontractor shall furnish all labor, materials, equipment, and supervision necessary to complete the following work in accordance with the Contract Documents:

1.1 Complete electrical installation for Building A (45,000 SF retail space) including:
- Main electrical service (2000A, 480/277V, 3-phase)
- All branch circuit wiring and devices
- LED lighting package per architectural specifications
- Fire alarm system rough-in (devices by others)
- Data/telecom conduit and pull strings
- Emergency generator connections
- Parking lot lighting (32 pole-mounted LED fixtures)

1.2 Work specifically EXCLUDED:
- Fire alarm devices and programming
- Security system
- Audio/visual systems
- Tenant finish work (future phase)

## ARTICLE 3: SCHEDULE

3.1 **Commencement Date:** February 1, 2026

3.2 **Substantial Completion:** July 15, 2026

3.3 **Liquidated Damages:** Subcontractor agrees to pay General Contractor the sum of **$2,500 per calendar day** for each day Substantial Completion extends beyond July 15, 2026, not as a penalty but as liquidated damages representing a genuine pre-estimate of loss.

3.4 **Acceleration:** If Subcontractor falls behind schedule, General Contractor may direct Subcontractor to accelerate the Work at Subcontractor's sole cost and expense, including overtime, additional crews, or other measures deemed necessary.

3.5 **Schedule Changes:** General Contractor reserves the right to modify the project schedule at any time. Subcontractor shall not be entitled to additional compensation for schedule changes unless such changes result in documented increased costs AND are approved in writing by General Contractor in advance.

---

## ARTICLE 4: PAYMENT TERMS

4.1 **Progress Payments:** Subcontractor shall submit monthly pay applications by the 25th of each month. General Contractor shall pay approved amounts within **45 days** after receipt of payment from Owner.

4.2 **Pay-If-Paid:** General Contractor's obligation to pay Subcontractor is expressly contingent upon General Contractor's receipt of corresponding payment from Owner. If Owner fails to pay General Contractor for any reason, General Contractor shall have no obligation to pay Subcontractor for that portion of the Work.

4.3 **Retainage:** General Contractor shall retain **10%** of each progress payment. Retainage shall be released within 60 days after final completion and acceptance of all Work, provided Subcontractor has submitted all required closeout documentation.

4.4 **Conditional Lien Waivers:** As a condition precedent to each payment, Subcontractor shall provide conditional lien waivers for the current payment and unconditional lien waivers for all prior payments.

---

## ARTICLE 5: CHANGES IN THE WORK

5.1 **Change Order Requirement:** No change in the Work shall be valid unless authorized by a written Change Order signed by General Contractor. Subcontractor expressly waives any claim for additional compensation for work performed without a signed Change Order.

5.2 **Pricing of Changes:** Change Order pricing shall be based on:
- Labor: Actual cost plus 10% markup
- Materials: Actual cost plus 10% markup
- Equipment: Actual cost plus 5% markup
- **No markup on markup** - Subcontractor's markup is deemed to include all overhead and profit

5.3 **Disputed Changes:** If Subcontractor disputes a Change Order price, Subcontractor shall nevertheless proceed with the Work as directed. Failure to proceed shall constitute a material breach.

5.4 **Constructive Changes:** Subcontractor waives any claim based on constructive changes, cardinal changes, or scope growth unless documented in a signed Change Order.

6.1 **Broad Form Indemnity:** To the fullest extent permitted by law, Subcontractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless General Contractor, Owner, Architect, and their respective officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to:

(a) The performance of the Work, regardless of whether caused in whole or in part by the negligence of General Contractor;

(b) Any breach of this Agreement by Subcontractor;

(c) Any violation of law by Subcontractor or its employees;

(d) Any claim by Subcontractor's employees, suppliers, or sub-subcontractors.

6.2 This indemnification obligation shall survive completion of the Work and termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 7: INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS

7.1 Subcontractor shall maintain the following insurance coverages:

- Commercial General Liability: $2,000,000 per occurrence / $4,000,000 aggregate
- Automobile Liability: $1,000,000 combined single limit
- Workers' Compensation: Statutory limits
- Employers' Liability: $1,000,000
- Umbrella/Excess: $5,000,000
- Professional Liability (if applicable): $1,000,000

7.2 General Contractor, Owner, and Architect shall be named as **Additional Insureds** on all liability policies.

7.3 All policies shall contain a **Waiver of Subrogation** in favor of General Contractor and Owner.

7.4 Subcontractor shall provide certificates of insurance prior to commencing Work. **Failure to maintain required insurance shall be grounds for immediate termination.**

## ARTICLE 8: WARRANTY

8.1 Subcontractor warrants all Work shall be free from defects in materials and workmanship for a period of **two (2) years** from Substantial Completion of the entire Project (not Subcontractor's Work).

8.2 Subcontractor shall repair or replace defective Work within **48 hours** of notification. If Subcontractor fails to respond within 48 hours, General Contractor may perform remedial work and back-charge Subcontractor at 150% of actual costs incurred.

8.3 This warranty is **in addition to** any manufacturer warranties, statutory warranties, or other warranties implied by law.

---

## ARTICLE 9: TERMINATION

9.1 **Termination for Convenience:** General Contractor may terminate this Agreement at any time for its convenience upon 5 days written notice. Upon such termination, Subcontractor shall be entitled only to payment for Work satisfactorily completed prior to termination. Subcontractor waives any claim for lost profits, lost overhead, or consequential damages.

9.2 **Termination for Cause:** General Contractor may terminate this Agreement immediately if Subcontractor:
- Fails to prosecute the Work diligently
- Fails to maintain required insurance
- Becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy
- Violates any provision of this Agreement

9.3 Upon termination for cause, General Contractor may complete the Work using others and charge Subcontractor for any excess costs.

---

## ARTICLE 10: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

10.1 **Mandatory Mediation:** Prior to initiating any legal action, the parties shall attempt to resolve disputes through mediation administered by the American Arbitration Association.

10.2 **Binding Arbitration:** Any dispute not resolved through mediation shall be settled by binding arbitration in accordance with the Construction Industry Rules of the American Arbitration Association. The arbitration shall take place in Austin, Texas.

10.3 **Attorneys' Fees:** The prevailing party in any dispute shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party.

10.4 **Continued Performance:** Subcontractor shall continue to perform the Work during any dispute. Failure to continue performance shall constitute a material breach and waiver of Subcontractor's claims.

## ARTICLE 11: GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 **Assignment:** Subcontractor shall not assign this Agreement or any portion thereof without prior written consent of General Contractor.

11.2 **No Damages for Delay:** Subcontractor's sole remedy for delays caused by General Contractor or others shall be an extension of time. Subcontractor expressly waives any claim for delay damages, including lost productivity, extended overhead, or acceleration costs.

11.3 **Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

11.4 **Integration:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. No oral representations or prior negotiations shall modify this Agreement.

11.5 **Severability:** If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

11.6 **Waiver of Consequential Damages:** Subcontractor waives any claim for consequential, incidental, or punitive damages arising from this Agreement or the Work.
