The Problem

Contracts Designed to Protect the GC — Not You

GCs use templates written by their lawyers to shift risk onto subcontractors. Here's what they bury in the fine print:

Pay-If-Paid Clauses

You only get paid if the GC gets paid. Owner disputes and bankruptcies become your problem.

Lien Waiver Traps

Unconditional waivers required before payment. If their check bounces, you've signed away your rights.

Broad Indemnification

"Any and all claims" language means you cover the GC's negligence. Their mistake could end your business.

Retainage Without Release

10% held back with no specified release date. Your money sits in their account for months or years.

Impossible Change Orders

24-48 hour written notice requirements. Missing that window means extra work you can't bill for.

Liquidated Damages

$500-$1000/day penalties for delays, even ones you didn't cause. One clause can wipe out your profit.
